<p><p>Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS) treatments include various drug classes. Alkylating agents, such as azacitidine, modify DNA to inhibit cancer cell growth. Cytotoxic antibiotics, like idarubicin, target rapidly dividing cells, disrupting their function. Topoisomerase inhibitors, such as topotecan, interfere with DNA replication and repair processes, leading to cancer cell death. Additionally, other treatment options may include immunomodulatory drugs and supportive therapies. These diverse treatments aim to improve patient outcomes by addressing the underlying abnormalities of MDS.</p></p>
