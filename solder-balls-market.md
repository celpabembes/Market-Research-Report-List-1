<p><p>The solder balls market comprises two main types: lead solder balls and lead-free solder balls. Lead solder balls, traditionally used in electronic packaging, contain lead, which enhances conductivity and reliability but poses health and environmental risks. In contrast, lead-free solder balls have gained popularity due to regulatory pressures and growing awareness of environmental safety. These alternatives often utilize tin, silver, and copper, making them safer but sometimes compromising on thermal and mechanical properties compared to their lead-based counterparts.</p></p>
